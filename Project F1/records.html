Driver World Record

Most World Championships: 7 (Michael Schumacher and Lewis Hamilton)

These drivers share the record for the most Formula 1 World Championships, with each winning the championship title 7 times during their careers.

Team World Record

Most Consecutive Constructors' Championships: 5 (Scuderia Ferrari)

Scuderia Ferrari holds the record for winning the Constructors' Championship title for 5 consecutive years, showcasing their dominance in the sport.

Team Principal World Record

Longest-serving Team Principal: 41 years (Frank Williams)

Frank Williams holds the record for being the longest-serving Team Principal in Formula 1, contributing to the success of Williams Racing.

Driver World Record

Most Grand Slams (Pole, Win, Fastest Lap, Leading Every Lap) in a Season: 5 (Jim Clark)

Jim Clark set the record for achieving the Grand Slam in a single season by securing pole position, race win, fastest lap, and leading every lap in 5 races.

Driver World Record

Most Consecutive Wins: 9 (Alberto Ascari)

Alberto Ascari achieved the incredible feat of winning 9 consecutive races, a record that still stands as one of the longest winning streaks in F1 history.

Driver World Record

Most Pole Positions: 101 (Lewis Hamilton)

Lewis Hamilton holds the record for the most pole positions, showcasing his exceptional speed and qualifying prowess.

Driver World Record

Most Grand Slam Wins: 8 (Michael Schumacher)

Michael Schumacher achieved the Grand Slam (pole, race win, fastest lap, leading every lap) in 8 races during his illustrious career.

Team World Record

Most Points in a Season: 726 (Mercedes, 2020)

Mercedes set the record for the most points accumulated in a single season, highlighting their dominant performance throughout the year.

Driver World Record

Most Consecutive Points Finishes: 33 (Kimi Räikkönen)

Kimi Räikkönen holds the record for the most consecutive points finishes, showcasing his consistency and skill in earning points in races.

Driver World Record

Most Fastest Laps in a Season: 10 (Jim Clark and Michael Schumacher)

Both Jim Clark and Michael Schumacher share the record for the most fastest laps achieved in a single Formula 1 season.

Driver World Record

Youngest World Champion: 23 years, 134 days (Max Verstappen)

Max Verstappen achieved the record for being the youngest driver to win the Formula 1 World Championship at the age of 23 years and 134 days.

Driver World Record

Oldest World Champion: 46 years, 41 days (Juan Manuel Fangio)

Juan Manuel Fangio holds the record for being the oldest driver to win the Formula 1 World Championship at the age of 46 years and 41 days.

Driver World Record

Most Consecutive Podiums: 19 (Michael Schumacher)

Michael Schumacher achieved the record for the most consecutive podium finishes, showcasing his consistency at the top of the field.

Driver World Record

Most Wins at a Single Grand Prix: 8 (Michael Schumacher at French Grand Prix)

Michael Schumacher holds the record for the most wins at a single Grand Prix, with 8 victories at the French Grand Prix.

Driver World Record

Most Wins at Different Circuits: 23 (Lewis Hamilton)

Lewis Hamilton holds the record for the most wins at different Formula 1 circuits, showcasing his adaptability and skill on various tracks.
